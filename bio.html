Hi, I’m Belle

Building, investing, and making music — always with curiosity.

Hi, I’m Belle — born and raised in Bangkok, Thailand, and now based in San Francisco. I moved to California for UC Berkeley, graduated from Cal, and live here full-time.

I run growth at Hyperbolic Labs. Across roles at General Catalyst, Pantera Capital, Berkeley Frontier Fund, and Third Sphere, I’ve built a sourcing engine grounded in technical pattern recognition and community—hosting 30+ curated founder gatherings, from AI/compute meetups and co-hosted hackathons to intimate dinners like The Compute Table Series that consistently convert into real dealflow.

I’m drawn to the intersection of AI, Data, infrastructure, and markets—especially the layers that make AI systems actually work in production: model serving & inference systems, distributed training, scheduling & resource allocation, compute markets, and security/compliance + verifiability. I’m also highly interested in healthcare and biotech. Outside of tech, I’m forever a harpist: I perform, and my most memorable work was doing harp therapy at local hospitals in Bangkok. I also train taekwondo, play soccer, ski (I love snow so much since Thailand is basically always 100°F+ lol...), and collect toys (I was also a museum curator at a toy museum). In my free time, I’m usually listening to hip-hop and R&B, going to concerts, baking, or watching anime and K-dramas—fun a lot of the time, but also occasionally straight-up traumatizing (AOT and Chainsaw Man are works of art).
